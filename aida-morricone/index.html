Ennio Morricone

  • Born 10 Nov 1928- 6 Jul 2020 in Rome
  • Was an Italian composer, orchestrator, conductor and trumpter, who wrote music in different style, like film music, classical, jazz, pop and rock.
  • He is considered the most greatest film composers scores with 500 (400 scores for film and television, 100 scores for classical music) of all time, mostly for the Western movies.

Early Life and Education

  • Morricone was born in Rome, son of Libera and Mario Morricone.
  • Mario Morricone was a professional trumpeter, who performend in music orchestras. Was an Italian composer, orchestrator, conductor and trumpter, who wrote music in different style, like film music, classical, jazz, pop and rock.
  • Was Morricoe's father to taught him to read music and play severals instruments (trumpet, violin, piano).
  • He wrote his first composition at six years old.
  • At age of 12, he entered Conservatory of Santa Cecilia, to take trumpet lessons, composition and choral music.
  • In 1956, Morricone started to play in jazz band and arrange pop songs for Italian singers.
  • In 1984, he conducted the Orchestre National des Pays de la Loire in the French hall.

Orchestra

Ennio Morricone has conducted more than 11 orchestras worldwide including the Orchestra of the Santa Cecilia Academy for many symphonic seasons.

He performed in many orchestras like as Orchestre national des Pays de la Loire, National Orchestra of Belgium, Orchestra Rome ecct.

He performed also in World Tour started in 2001 and finished in 2019. Morricone in concert normally had between 160-180 musicians, vocalists under his baton and performing multiple genere crossing collections music. In 2019 I had the pleasure of seeing one of his concerts at the arena of Verona and it was a unique and unrepeatable experience.

Influence

Morricone has been an influential for many singers such as Metallica (The Ecstasy of Gold), Hans Zimmer, Radiohead, Muse.

In 2007, the tribute album ''We all Love Ennio Morricoe'' was released, featuring performances by various artists, including Andrea Bocelli, Celine Dion, Bruce Springsteen and Metallica.

In 2016 at Morricone is had attributed the star in the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

Awards

In 2007, he received the ''Academy Honorary Award'' for his contributions to the art of film music.

He was nominated for six Oscars, and in 2016, received his only competitive Academy Award for his score to Quentin Tarantino's film "The Hateful Eight".

His other achievements include three Golden Globes, six Grammy Awards, six BAFTAs, ten David de Donatello, eleven Nastro D'Argento, two European Film Award, the Golden Lion of Venice, Honorary Award, and the Polar Music Prize in 2010.
